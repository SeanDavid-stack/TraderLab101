Mean Reversion Trade

Structured checklist — fractal alignment required

Core concept: Market seeks fair price (DVPOC/HVN) in all fractals. Like a Matryoshka doll — smaller fractals nest within bigger ones, same mechanics repeat at every scale. Trade from micro-fractal LVN back toward DVPOC, aligned with HTF direction. When price extends to an LVN (outside edge), it tends to revert to the mean.

Mean Reversion (MR)
Matryoshka Concept
Smaller fractals nest within bigger ones. Market repeats at every scale. Too high or too low in a small fractal → returns to check fair price in the larger fractal. Execute in micro structure aligned with HTF.
Qualifications
✓ Market in balance / rotational
✓ Price trades into LVN (outside edge)
✓ Brief pause — micro fractal forms
✓ HTF context supports reversion
✓ Triggering structure present
Entry Sequence
① Break of fractal balance (trigger)
② Pullback to retest fractal edge
③ Entry: 1 tick above edge or HVN
④ Stop: 3 ticks below structure
DO NOT MOVE STOP
Targets
PRIMARY: DVPOC — the mean
Scale: VWAP (obstacle if between entry/DVPOC)
Scale: MID (obstacle if between entry/DVPOC)
Scale: VHVN — prior fair price
Extension: Opposite LVN (full rotation)
Risk Rules
Scale to risk neutral — 1st contract buys stop
Stop stays 3 ticks below structure
DO NOT move stop after scaling
Track: probability of risk neutral before full stop
Then: track probability of reaching DVPOC
Initial Balance Failure (IBF)
Market breaks outside IB, exhausts, and comes back inside. The stops that piled up behind the breakout move become fuel to trade back to retail (mean). Can be used to realign with HTF trend at the opposite IB extreme.
📊 After breaking IBH/IBL: VPOC return 89.8% · VWAP return 71.4% · Opposite dVAH/L 40.8% · ~30% chance of taking out opposite IB side (neutral day)
Qualifications
✓ Market in balance (or HTF trend opposite extreme)
✓ IB broken but fails to stabilize outside
✓ Price comes back inside IB range
✓ Triggering structure on return
✓ Range to scale in front of high volume
Targets
MID — scale / anticipate reaction
VWAP — scale / anticipate reaction
VPOC — primary mean reversion target
VAH/L · IBH/L — extension targets
VPOC Shift
DVPOC migrates to new location — market declaring a new potential fair price. Accept = extend. Reject = return to VHVN. Shift must be to a new HVN with a clear LVN separating the two.
✗ Market in balance (tight VWAP/MID/VPOC cluster)  ✗ Micro VPOC shifts  ✗ No clear LVN separation  ✗ Price crosses back through VPOC into MR

③ MR Around

BAR — Break and Retest
Order flow pattern at micro consolidations. Market goes into consolidation → volume builds a node in the profile → aggressive break away → pullback to check the high volume left behind. If price rejects the fractal, trade the continuation.
Qualifications
✓ Consolidation visible in volume profile (node)
✓ Aggressive trigger — volume + delta push price out
✓ Stops of weak retail taken on the break
✓ Pullback to check micro volume (HVN of fractal)
✓ Exhaustion / absorption / sweep on retest
Fractal Rejection
Edge rejection: Price rejects at fractal boundary → market saying "entire fractal is unfair"
HVN check: Price re-enters fractal → checks the HVN (fair price of that fractal)
Longer consolidation = more stops = better fuel
This trade works in all fractals and timeframes — same behavior repeats at every scale. No structured location needed — the consolidation IS the structure.
MID > VWAP > VPOC
Retail traders pile stops at MID and VWAP. After an exhaustion move, market pulls back to these levels to pick the stops — then continues in the dominant direction. Use this to realign with trend when market transitions from directional to rotational.
Qualifications
✓ Market out of balance → transitions to balance in smaller fractal
✓ Stop pick at MID / VWAP — weak players taken out
✓ Triggering structure after stop pick
✓ Range to scale in front of high volume
✓ Targets identified
Targets
PRIMARY: VPOC — mean revert to fair price
Extension: Opposite side of range
Counter rotation → stop pick → back to VPOC
This is a mean reversion trade used to realign with trend
VHVN > VPOC
When the VPOC shifts, the previous fair price (VHVN) may no longer be fair. If market cannot hold the new VPOC and returns to check the VHVN, this is an opportunity to trade a mean reversion back to the new fair price — realigning with trend.
Qualifications
✓ Market in balance
✓ VPOC has shifted — clear LVN between VHVN and new VPOC
✓ Price returns to check VHVN
✓ Triggering structure at VHVN
✓ Range to scale in front of high volume
Targets
PRIMARY: New VPOC — return to new fair price
VHVN acts like any HVN — expect reaction
If VHVN is unfair → trade back to VPOC
Extension: HTF targets in alignment with trend
The VHVN acts just like a VPOC, MCVPOC, or HVN — it's a previous area of high volume agreement. Treat it as a structural level with the same entry mechanics.
Triggering Structures — the entry signal. Never chase. Wait for the pullback.
Triggering structures appear in all timeframes and fractals. Identify the pattern in the micro structure to get in alignment with the HTF context. The pullback is the entry — not the break.
Type 1 — LONG
Chop → Break High → Pullback → Long
Consolidation forms. Break high signals price is too low. Pullback checks the fractal edge/HVN. If it holds — price is still too low. Enter long on the retest.
Type 2 — SHORT
Chop → Break Low → Pullback → Short
Consolidation forms. Break low signals price is too high. Pullback checks the fractal edge/HVN. If it holds as resistance — price is still too high. Enter short on the retest.
Type 3 — LONG stop sweep → continuation
Chop → Break Low → Fail → Break High → Pullback → Long
False break below stops the sellers. Buyers absorb. Price crosses back above fair value — it's now too low. Break high confirms. Pullback to volume for entry.
Type 4 — SHORT stop sweep → continuation
Chop → Break High → Fail → Break Low → Pullback → Short
False break above stops the buyers. Sellers absorb. Price crosses back below fair value — it's now too high. Break low confirms. Pullback to volume for entry.
The pullback is the entry — not the initial break. If you chase the break you are entering after the signal has already fired. Wait for price to return and check the fractal edge.
Stop placement: At minimum 3 ticks behind the structure the pullback is checking. As experience grows, stop distance can become narrower or wider based on the full context of the setup — fractal size, nearby liquidity, and how price is behaving at the level. The 3-tick rule is the floor, not the ceiling.
THE OUTCOME OF EACH INDIVIDUAL TRADE WILL BE RANDOM.
If conditions are not met — pass on the trade. FOMO and bad execution come from skipping the process. A consistent process is how you measure and extract edge from the market. When price comes into a level: laser focus, follow the process, when ALL conditions are met — take your trade.

Mean Reversion (MR) LVN → DVPOC · Matryoshka fractals · balanced context ▼
Explanation

The market is always trying to figure out what is too low, too high, and what is fair in all fractals and time frames. If we relate fractals to a Matryoshka doll (Russian nesting doll), they are micro structure auctions inside of bigger auctions. Smaller fractals are found within bigger fractals and the market repeats itself in all fractals.

The market tends to return to the mean or fair price in all fractals. If market gets too high or too low in a smaller fractal, it can return to check the fair price in the higher fractal. We can use this process to align with the HTF context by executing trades in the smaller fractals or micro structure.

Setup

This trade qualifies when the market is in a rotational context — either balanced context for the day, or when we are in a trend configuration and market makes a pause. We are looking to trade from the outside edge / LVN back to the mean and possibly out the other side.

Qualifications
Market in balance
Market trades into LVN (outside edge of distribution)
Market makes a brief pause — consolidation / micro fractal forms
Triggering structure — the break of the fractal balance
Pullback to check the fractal edge or HVN after trigger — if price was unfair when we left, buyers/sellers who were there before may return
Stop: 3 ticks behind entry structure — if market picks off stops, the stronger side may push price back toward the mean
Scale to risk neutral — first contract buys the stop. DO NOT MOVE YOUR STOP after scaling
Targets
PRIMARY: DVPOC — the mean / highest volume for the day
VWAP — scaling location, anticipate retail reaction (obstacle if between entry and DVPOC)
MID — scaling location, anticipate retail reaction (obstacle if between entry and DVPOC)
VHVNs — previous fair price levels, expect retail stops above/below. If checking from above → might still be too low. From below → might still be too high
Extension — through DVPOC, rotate to opposite side of daily fractal
DO NOT MOVE YOUR STOP. The stop stays 3 ticks under the structure. This is critical for education and development. Track two probabilities: (1) getting risk neutral before full stop-out, (2) reaching DVPOC after risk neutral.
Entry: limit order 1 tick in front of the edge of the fractal or 1 tick in front of the HVN of the fractal. This needs to be vetted to determine your edge and must align with your psychology.
IBC — Initial Balance Continuation IB break + retest + range extension · out of balance ▼
Explanation

In the first hour of trading there is a lot of volume that goes into the market — this creates the Initial Balance range. If we get outside of the IB range, this is range extension and we might anticipate that HTF traders are active. When we break the IB, we want to see new participants coming in and do not want to see the advancing army exhaust out.

We anticipate stops outside both edges of the IB because this is the current high and low of the day — longs have stops below, shorts have stops above. We ideally want to see a retest of the level that was broken based on support and resistance. The IBL becomes support when we break above, resistance when we break below.

Setup

When price breaks out of IB, stops may be piled up on the edges. Once taken out, watch for aggressive participation. Price may pull back to check the IB level — this creates the trade opportunity. But remember: each retest piles more stops and the structure can weaken. If price cannot continue outside IB, watch for the IBF setup instead.

The key is taking this trade in the proper context: trending or out of balance. This level can be used similar to any other HVN.

Qualifications

IBF — Initial Balance Failure break + exhaust + back inside · stops become fuel ▼
Explanation

When the market is in balance, the IB low and high act as the outside edge of a distribution where volume tapers off. If market is in balance it tends to come back to check the high volume price. When price trades into the high or low of balance range, stops pile up behind the movement. If we exhaust, the other side can take control and pull back to retail — all those stops that were piling up now become fuel for the move back.

This trade can be used to realign with trend. For example if the trend is up, and we see an IBF trigger at the low, we can take the counter-trend trade back into balance, picking off stops at MID, VWAP, and VPOC.

Setup

If the market cannot get outside of balance, or gets outside, exhausts, and comes back in — look for triggering structure and order flow to trade into the stops piling up behind the price movement. The stops are the fuel to trade back to retail. If we get back to retail we can trade to the other side of value and the other side of IB, or just pick the retail stops and push back.

Qualifications
Market in balance — or if trend is one direction, take at opposite extreme (e.g., market is long and selling off → get involved if IB breaks and fails to stabilize outside)

BAR — Break and Retest micro consolidation · order flow pattern · all fractals ▼
Explanation

This trade is based on an order flow pattern that may be used when there is not a structured location. It is the same triggering behavior we see at structured locations but happens within a fractal. It is based on the fact that the market will continually come back to check high volume in all time frames and fractals.

When the market goes into a micro consolidation, protective stops are placed on each outside edge. The buying and selling criss-crossing back and forth makes a consolidation in the volume profile. Think of a micro consolidation as a high volume location with an outside edge or LVN that tapers off at the edges of the distribution. If we break away from this area, the market is saying this price is now unfair — and we may come back to check it.

Setup

Market goes into consolidation — the longer the consolidation, the better the trade opportunity due to more stops as fuel. When the market breaks out of the distribution, this is the trigger — we should see increased volume in the delta, aggressive market orders pushing price out, and stops of weak retail taken out. After the trigger, we look for a pullback to the micro volume left behind. If this price is unfair, we can reject it and take the trade. On the retest, look for exhaustion, absorption, sweeps of the book, and order flow.

Qualifications
Consolidation of volume — must be able to see this node in the volume profile
Trigger — aggressive order flow and volume that moves price outside the consolidation
Pullback — after trigger, market must pull back to check the volume
Fractal Rejection — two key places: the edge of the fractal and the HVN of the fractal
Edge rejection: When we left the fractal, market was saying price is unfair. If we come back and reject at the edge, the market is saying it may be rejecting the entire fractal. HVN check: If we come back into the fractal, anticipate the market will check the HVN — that's where retail decided fair price was, visible as the highest volume level in the fractal.

Opening Trade open type detection · gap vs in-range · responsive vs impulsive ▼
Explanation

Executed at or near the open based on the opening context flowchart. The open type determines bias and initial direction. All opens are classified as either Gap (HOR/LOR — out of range) or In Range (HIR-OV/LIR-OV — out of value, HIR-IV/LIR-IV — in value near extreme, IR-IV — in value).

Gap Opens (HOR / LOR)
Responsive? → Price attempts to return toward prior range/value. Fade back into balance.
Impulsive? → Market continues driving in gap direction. Trade direction of imbalance.
Gap-and-Go? → Trend day, unlimited range potential.
In Range Opens (HIR-OV / LIR-OV / HIR-IV / LIR-IV / IR-IV)
IR-IV → Neutral day. Trade VA extremes, mean revert. Market is in balance.
HIR-OV / LIR-OV → Watch for range breakout. If breaking range → trade direction, unlimited.
Key levels: ONVPOC · pVAH · pVAL · pClose · ONH · ONL · pMID · Settlement · NVPOC. The opening context flowchart (Open Context tab) walks through the decision tree for each open type.
Triggering Structures the entry signal · 4 types · never chase the break ▼
Explanation

Triggering structures appear in all timeframes and fractals. Identify the pattern in the micro structure to get in alignment with the HTF context. The pullback is the entry — not the break.

Type 1 — LONG
Chop → Break High → Pullback → Long
Consolidation forms. Break high signals price is too low. Pullback checks the fractal edge/HVN. If it holds — price is still too low. Enter long on the retest.
Type 2 — SHORT
Chop → Break Low → Pullback → Short
Consolidation forms. Break low signals price is too high. Pullback checks the fractal edge/HVN. If it holds as resistance — price is still too high. Enter short on the retest.
Type 3 — LONG (Sweep)
Chop → Sweep Low → Break High → Pullback → Long
False break low takes out stops (stop sweep). Price recovers, breaks high on real conviction. Pullback entry after the break high — swept stops become fuel.
Type 4 — SHORT (Sweep)
Chop → Sweep High → Break Low → Pullback → Short
False break high takes out stops (stop sweep). Price rejects, breaks low on real conviction. Pullback entry after the break low — swept stops become fuel.
Core Concepts — Auction Market Theory balance · fractals · volume profile · fair price ▼
Balance vs Imbalance
In Balance: Market is rotational. Trading inside previous range/value. VWAP/MID/VPOC tight cluster. Mean reversion setups dominate (MR, IBF, VHVN).
Out of Balance: Market is directional/trending. Outside previous range. IBC, VPOC Shift, Opening Trade setups dominate.
Fractals (Matryoshka)
Smaller auctions nest within larger ones, like Russian nesting dolls. The same patterns repeat at every scale. Execute trades in smaller fractals aligned with the HTF / larger fractal context.
Key Price Levels
VPOC — highest volume price (fair). VWAP — volume-weighted avg. MID — midpoint of range. HVN/LVN — high/low volume nodes. VA — value area (70% of volume). VHVN — variable HVN, previous fair price. NVPOC — naked VPOC from a prior session.
Risk Management
Stop: 3 ticks behind entry structure — always.
Scale 1: Risk neutral — first contract buys the stop.
DO NOT MOVE YOUR STOP after scaling.
Track: P(risk neutral) and P(target) to calculate your edge.
Context supersedes statistics — no exceptions. All inputs to your trade plan require prioritization. The primary focus should never be the stats; it's the context. When the two are in conflict, context supersedes statistics. As the market story changes, so does the value of every stat.
